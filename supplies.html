explore the wonderful world of sewing supplies

You don't have to buy supplies such as fabric, machines,
or notions, but should you wish to bring your own for more personalised or
complex projects, your wonderful secretary has compiled a list of the
best places to go for them. Enjoy!

Walker’s Fabric

Where: Indoor Market/Market Pl
Durham City Centre, DH1 3NG

They are an Aladdin's Cave for all your haberdashery needs. From stocking fabrics for dressmaking and quilting, to buttons, ribbons and elastics. The selection is quite small in comparison to some of the others on the list, but it's a great little gem!

Durham Market Stall 69-70

Where: Indoor Market/Market Pl
Durham City Centre, DH1 3NG

They have a slightly limited selection but products are very cheap and the staff are really friendly. They sell old patterns and magazines for 50p each - a bargain! It's great for quilt squares and other smaller cuts, as well as offering a small variety of cotton prints and other types of fabric.

http://www.sewscrumptious.co.uk/

Lots of gorgeous unique prints and designer names. Self-described as “mid-price”. The site is good for a special treat or on sale, but can get pricey quickly. However, shipping is fast and very cheap.

http://www.myfabrics.co.uk

Basically a fabric “supermarket”, it has everything from designer names to crazy novelty prints to the most basic fabric needs in every kind of material. Prices vary tremendously, however most are priced by the metre. Easy to navigate website and shipping is reasonably priced, but can be slow.

http://www.cheapfabrics.co.uk

Exactly what it sounds like, a cheap fabric emporium: there is even a selection of “under £2.50”! They have most everything you would need, however, the site is difficult to use. Make sure you go in knowing exactly what you’re looking for.
